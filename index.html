    My Favorite Rock Band!

    Back to Top

    Styx

    My first ever Rock Concert I saw in person was on June 30, 1983 in Virginia. It was at the then Norfolk Scope Convention Hall in Norfolk. The band was Styx. (my original ticket stub above from that concert.) It was the "Kilroy was Here" Tour and I went with my parents and my older brother and sister. In those days they had General Admission seating which meant you were allowed to "upgrade" your ticket by running up to the stage as fast as you could to beat the other fans who wanted to get as close as possible too. My older brother and sister and I were next to the band by the front middle part of the stage and watched the entire show with total delight. I was 13!!! üòç The Mr. Roboto song and performance was awesome to me as it transitioned from the short opening movie (about reopening the Paradise Theater) to the songs being now sung as part of the regular concert! Mr. Roboto by Dennis DeYoung was the opening song as it followed with that cool theme of Dennis DeYoung and Tommy Shaw as freedom fighters (like the Star Wars movies of that day) fighting to keep Rock N Roll alive! James "J.Y." Young was the "bad guy" in this storyline. A great experience! My brother even high-fived Tommy Shaw during the concert as Tommy was rocking out playing guitar and reaching out to the fans during one song! Wow!!!!! üòâ

    Even though today the band has mixed feelings about this show (they broke up and split into two, the Tommy Shaw/"J.Y." side vs the Dennis DeYoung side, now with two different perspectives on how things went...), it will always remain as a highlight musically in my life because it made me love them as a group even more! Yes, from this concert I saw in person! In my humble opinion, a lot of kids like me, I was 13, became Styx fans from these tour songs and how they showcased it to the public on stage. I agree with Dennis DeYoung on this, but I respect the hell out of Tommy Shaw and James "J.Y." Young (in general) also as they weren't giant fans of this type of show! They just wanted to play Rock N Roll and not do themed concerts which was very popular in those days which Dennis DeYoung wanted to do and liked. I guess we all will agree to disagree! There were many reasons why they broke up. This I believe was one example of it, but their music will live forever!

    About the Band Styx

    Styx is an American rock band formed in Chicago, Illinois, in 1972. They are known for blending melodic hard rock guitar with acoustic guitar, synthesizers mixed with acoustic piano, upbeat tracks with power ballads, and incorporating elements of international musical theatre. The band established themselves with a progressive rock sound during the 1970s, and began to incorporate pop rock and soft rock elements in the 1980s. (From Wikipedia)
